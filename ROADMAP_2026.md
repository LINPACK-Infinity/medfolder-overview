# ROADMAP 2026

## Development Roadmap — 2026

This roadmap reflects the current internal technical direction of MEDFOLDER as of April 2026.

The focus remains on controlled prototype hardening, architectural refinement and preparation for realistic pilot readiness rather than premature product scaling.

---

## Q2 2026 — Extraction Hardening and Structural Stability

Primary focus:

- improve extraction robustness across heterogeneous medical PDFs  
- stabilize OCR fallback handling  
- refine diagnosis priority logic  
- improve medication normalization consistency  
- reduce extraction noise in difficult document layouts  

Architectural focus:

- continue decomposition of core monolith structures  
- isolate document preprocessing logic  
- improve internal pipeline observability  

Expected outcome:

A more stable extraction layer across variable document quality and layout complexity.

---

## Q3 2026 — Longitudinal Reliability and Review Depth

Primary focus:

- strengthen cross-document timeline consistency  
- improve diagnosis conflict handling  
- refine medication conflict visibility  
- harden evidence-linked chronology generation  

Review focus:

- expand correction workflows  
- improve review editor precision  
- strengthen traceability between extraction and source evidence  

Expected outcome:

More reliable longitudinal reconstruction under multi-document conditions.

---

## Q4 2026 — Pilot Preparation and Controlled External Validation

Primary focus:

- reduce remaining high-risk architectural bottlenecks  
- improve runtime predictability  
- prepare stable demonstration scenarios  
- define pilot-ready controlled workflows  

Validation focus:

- structured internal test cases  
- controlled external feedback  
- realistic workflow evaluation in practical medical document scenarios  

Expected outcome:

A technically credible prototype suitable for early controlled pilot discussion.

---

## Cross-Year Strategic Direction

Throughout 2026 the central principle remains:

- privacy-first processing  
- local-first architecture  
- explainable evidence linkage  
- no dependence on opaque external medical cloud inference  

The goal is not rapid scaling, but controlled technical credibility.

---

## Current Constraint Awareness

Several core areas remain active technical bottlenecks:

- monolith reduction  
- runtime complexity  
- extraction variance under heterogeneous source material  
- review-grade robustness  

These remain explicit engineering priorities during all roadmap phases.
